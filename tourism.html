Tourism Places



DAMAI BEACH

Damai Beach in Santubong, Sarawak, is a stunning tropical getaway known for its pristine sandy shores, clear waters, and breathtaking sunsets. Nestled between Mount Santubong's lush rainforests and the South China Sea, this beach offers a mix of relaxation and adventure. Visitors can enjoy activities like snorkeling, hiking, and wildlife cruises, and explore local culture at the nearby Sarawak Cultural Village. Just 35 kilometers from Kuching, Damai Beach is easily accessible and provides a range of accommodations, making it a perfect destination for all types of travelers.

MOUNT SANTUBONG

Mount Santubong in Kampung Santubong, Sarawak, is a stunning destination renowned for its scenic beauty, rich biodiversity, and cultural significance. Standing at 810 meters, it offers challenging hiking trails with panoramic views of the South China Sea and surrounding rainforests. The area is also steeped in local legends and cultural heritage, highlighted by the nearby Sarawak Cultural Village. Whether you're into hiking, nature, or culture, Mount Santubong provides a memorable experience for all visitors.

SANTUBONG NATIONAL PARK

Santubong National Park in Sarawak, Malaysia, is a beautiful natural sanctuary featuring the majestic Mount Santubong, which offers challenging hiking trails and stunning views. The park is rich in biodiversity, home to rare plants, exotic birds, and unique wildlife. Close to the Sarawak Cultural Village, it also provides insights into local indigenous cultures and is steeped in local myths and legends. Easily accessible from Kuching, the park is an ideal destination for nature lovers and cultural enthusiasts.

SARAWAK CULTURAL VILLAGE

The Sarawak Cultural Village is located at the foot of Mount Santubong and comprises 17 acres. Despite its small size, the Cultural Village functions as a living museum, allowing visitors to observe Sarawak's multiculturalism firsthand. The cultural town has a tranquil atmosphere and stunning scenery. There are traditional longhouses and varied forms of homes for Sarawak's seven primary ethnic groups: Ulu, Punam, Malano, Iban, Bidayu, Malay, and Chinese. They are all constructed from natural materials such as salt wood, bamboo, rattan, and yada leaves, giving the impression of a rustic community. Each house in this "living museum" features ethnic artists in traditional clothes working on handicrafts and explaining their way of life to guests, and there are frequent demonstrations of handicraft making as well as dance and music performances by many ethnic groups in the village. A visit to the Sarawak Cultural Village is a cultural journey that allow stourists to deeply experience Sarawak's rich cultural history. More about this place

GUNUNG GADING NATIONAL PARK

Gunung Gading National Park is located in Sarawak, Malaysia, approximately 56 kilometers from Kuching, and is known for being the home of the Rafflesia, the world's largest flower. The park covers an area of about 4,100 hectares and is characterized by rugged terrain, dense rainforest, and numerous streams. The main attraction of Gunung Gading National Park is the Rafflesia, a parasitic plant that can grow up to one meter in diameter and is famous for its foul-smelling odor. Visitors to the park can embark on guided treks led by park rangers to see these rare flowers in bloom, although timing can be unpredictable as Rafflesias bloom for only a few days. Apart from the Rafflesia, the park offers several hiking trails of varying difficulties, leading to scenic waterfalls such as the spectacular seven-tiered waterfall known as Waterfall No. 7.
