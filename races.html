Races In Kampung Santubong

Kampung Santubong in Sarawak, Malaysia, is a vibrant village that is home to a diverse population representing various ethnic groups. The primary ethnic communities in Kampung Santubong include Iban, Orang Kayan, Bidayuh and Melanau.


Iban

Iban community in Kampung Santubong, Sarawak, is one of the largest indigenous groups in the region, known for their rich cultural heritage and traditions. They are famous for their elaborate rituals, traditional longhouses, and the vibrant Ngajat dance. The Gawai Dayak festival, celebrating the harvest season, is a major cultural event for the Iban people. Traditionally, the Iban are farmers and hunters, although many have now ventured into modern occupations and businesses. The Iban language is widely spoken within the community, alongside Malay and English, preserving their unique identity and cultural practices in Kampung Santubong.

Orang Kayan

The Orang Kayan community in Kampung Santubong, Sarawak, is distinguished by its rich cultural heritage, which includes intricate beadwork, traditional tattooing, and the practice of elaborate rituals and festivals. Traditionally, the Kayan people are skilled in agriculture, fishing, and crafts such as weaving and metalwork, though many have now embraced modern professions. The Kayan language, along with Malay and English, is spoken within the community, helping to preserve their distinct identity and customs in Kampung Santubong.

Bidayuh

The Bidayuh community in Kampung Santubong is known for its unique culture, which includes traditional bamboo bridges, music, and dance, with the Gawai Padi festival celebrating the rice harvest being a significant event. Traditionally agriculturalists, the Bidayuh grow crops such as rice and pepper, though many have also ventured into other professions. The Bidayuh language, with its several dialects, is commonly spoken within the community, while Malay and English are also widely understood.

Melanau

The Melanau community in Kampung Santubong, Sarawak, is known for its distinct cultural heritage, which includes traditional fishing practices, the making of sago, and the annual Kaul Festival, a ritual of purification and thanksgiving. Traditionally, the Melanau are fishermen and sago producers, although many have diversified into other professions. The Melanau language is widely spoken within the community, alongside Malay and English, preserving their unique identity and customs in Kampung Santubong.
